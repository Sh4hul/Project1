Abstract

Project Overview

An IoT assistive device combining tongue-based mobility control with continuous vital sign monitoring.

Problem Statement

Patients paralysed below the neck — from quadriplegia, spinal cord injuries, or muscular dystrophy — cannot operate conventional joystick wheelchairs. Existing solutions like sip-and-puff and eye-tracking systems are expensive, complex, and tiring for long-term use.

The human tongue remains unaffected in most high-level paralysis cases. This project exploits that to create a simple, reliable, and low-cost control interface embedded inside a dental aligner.

Solution

Two TTP223 capacitive touch sensors inside a dental aligner detect tongue or teeth pressure. Signals are processed by a headset-mounted ESP32, which wirelessly transmits commands to a second ESP32 on the wheelchair via ESP-NOW.

The wheelchair ESP32 drives an L298N motor driver for movement and simultaneously monitors the user's body temperature (DS18B20) and pulse/SpO₂ (MAX30100), broadcasting data over Wi-Fi to a live web dashboard.

Operation

How It Works

Four sequential stages from tongue pressure to wheelchair movement.

— 01
👅

Tongue Input

TTP223 sensors in dental aligner detect tongue pressure. Left, right, or both — three commands via two sensors.

— 02
📡

Headset ESP32

Reads touch + DS18B20 + MAX30100 data, packs into a struct, transmits wirelessly via ESP-NOW at 1 Hz.

— 03
⚙️

Motor Control

Wheelchair ESP32 decodes command, drives L298N H-bridge: Left · Right · Forward · Stop via GPIO logic.

— 04
🌐

Web Dashboard

ESP32 hotspot hosts a live page. Phone connects to view temperature, heart rate, SpO₂ in real time.

Evaluation

Advantages & Limitations

Strengths of the approach and areas that require further development.

◼

Hands-Free Control

Patients paralysed below neck can operate without arm or hand movement.

◼

Easy Operation

3-command interface: Left, Right, Forward — minimal training required.

◼

Wireless

ESP-NOW ensures zero physical link between headset and wheelchair base.

◼

Health Monitoring

Continuous vital tracking enables emergency detection and safety alerts.

◼

Low Cost

Off-the-shelf modules keep total BOM cost accessible for all patients.

◼

Web Dashboard

Any smartphone can view live health data — no dedicated app required.

◻

Battery Maintenance

Regular charging of 18650 cells required for consistent performance.

◻

Wi-Fi Range

ESP-NOW range is limited to ~200m; walls and obstacles reduce reliability.

◻

No Obstacle Avoidance

Current version has no autonomous collision detection or avoidance.

Future

Future Scope

Planned upgrades to transform the prototype into a production-ready device.

01

Conclusion

Technology in Service
of Human Dignity

The Smart Tongue-Controlled Wheelchair proves that affordable IoT components can meaningfully restore independence for people with severe physical limitations. By unifying tongue-based input, wireless communication, motor control, and real-time health monitoring into a single compact system, this project delivers a clinically relevant, reproducible, and extensible assistive device.
